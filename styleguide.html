Typography

Typography makes the You Have Mail visual language readable, legible, and appealing through unifying type techniques and rules.

Typeface adds dimension and hierarchy to words.

The right typeface can help bring the content to life on the page. The Helvetica Neue font family is the standard typeface used to communicate the You Have Mail brand message.

A Typestyle is a format or treatment performed on characters.

These are the official You Have Mail typefaces meant for all the digital communications.

Headline

Page headers (or H1s) provide the title of the page or screen to users so they can quickly understand the content or tool available on that page.

H1 Headline: You Have Mail Blue #333366

Font: Roboto

Style: 30px, Initial Caps

Subhead

Subheads (or H2s) provide context to the user as they navigate through a content page or application flow. They help draw the user's eyes to each new chunk of content or step for using a tool.

Subhead: You Have Mail Blue #333366

Font: Roboto

Style: 19px, sentence case

Leading: 23px

Body Copy

Body copy provides additional information that supports the headline, subhead or page content.

Body Copy: Black #000000

Font: Roboto

Style: 14px, sentence case

Leading: 19px

Bullets

Both bulleted and numbered lists should be used to convey multiple amounts of similar information in quick, easy-to-read bursts. Lists should not have an ending period. Bulleted lists have blue bullets, while the numbers on an ordered list should be the same color as the list items themselves.

An example:

  • Deliver your message with a postage-paid response
  • Make your call-to-action clear
  • Include a reply envelope or postcard
